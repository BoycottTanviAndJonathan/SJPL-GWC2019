Global Warming

What Is It?

Global warming, also associated with climate change (the words used to describe the complex shifts now affecting the planet’s weather and climate systems), is a problem our planet is facing that began at the beginning of the industrial revolution. Global warming, to put it simply, is the gradual increase in the overall temperature in Earth’s atmosphere. Normally, the Earth’s climate is livable in because of the natural greenhouse effect. However, humans have been enhancing the greenhouse effect by adding loads of carbon dioxide to the atmosphere.

The greenhouse effect is warming that happens when certain gases in the Earth’s atmosphere trap heat. The gases let light in, but they keep heat from escaping out there. When sunlight shines onto the Earth’s surface, the energy is absorbed there and then proceeds to radiate back into the atmosphere as heat. In the atmosphere, the greenhouse molecules trap some of the heat and let the rest go to space. The more greenhouse gases concentrate in the atmosphere, the more heat gets locked up in the molecules, and causes global warming.

How Does It Affect Us?

Global warming is a major problem to us, and only affects more and more as time passes on. For starters, global warming is changing the climate faster than some living creatures can adapt to. These new and more unpredictable climate poses unique challenges to life. The weather is becoming more extreme, with intense major storms and more rain followed by longer, drier droughts. There can be changes in the ranges where crops grow and animals live, and global warming also provides a loss of water supplies. In addition, Earth’s remaining ice sheets (like Greenland or Antartica) are starting to melt. The extra water caused by the melting will add to the rising sea level. By 2050, scientists predict that the sea level will rise between one and 2.3 feet.

What Can We Do to Help?

There are many things people can do to stop global warming, and they aren’t very big efforts. In fact, most things you can do to help are guaranteed to be minor. Here is a list of things that you can do to help stop global warming:

  • Power homes with renewable energy
  • Invest in energy-efficient appliances
  • Reduce water waste
  • Reduce your carbon footprint
  • Reduce,reuse, recycle
  • Drive less(use trains, bikes, walk, etc)
  • Avoid products with a lot of packaging
  • Turn off lights in rooms you aren't using
  • Plant trees
  • Spread awareness
  • Buy energy efficient products
  • Use less hot water
  • Defrost energy efficient products
  • Cover pots while cooking
  • Use washing machines and dishwashers when they are full only
  • Take showers instead of baths
  • Buy locally grown and produced food/Buy fresh food instead of frozen
  • Eat less meat
  • Don't waste fuel for the car
  • Protect and conserve forests worlwide
Joining local groups focused on preventing global warming can also help give you more ideas on how to help prevent this looming threat.
